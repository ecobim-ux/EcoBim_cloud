02 . Process

A clean, predictable workflow.

Our four-phase workflow turns your design intent into a conflict-free, fully documented model on a timeline you can rely on.

01 / 04

Kickoff & BEP

We align on scope, LOD, naming conventions and a BIM Execution Plan. Every deliverable is agreed in writing before modeling starts.

02 / 04

Modeling

We model each discipline in Revit, Civil 3D or Tekla against your confirmed design intent, covering architectural, structural and MEP in a federated file.

03 / 04

Coordination

We run federation, clash tests and tracked BCF resolution in Navisworks until the model is fully clean. Every clash is logged with status and owner.

04 / 04

Delivery

We hand over drawings, takeoffs and as-built models, fully documented and auditable. Ongoing model management is available post-delivery.

Questions

Common questions.

What software do you deliver models in?

We model in Revit as our primary authoring tool and deliver in .rvt, .ifc, .nwd, .dwg or any format your workflow requires. All our federated models are run through Navisworks for clash coordination.

What LOD levels do you work to?

We work from LOD 100 concept massing through to LOD 400 fabrication-ready geometry. We agree the level in the BEP at kickoff and tie it to each discipline and project phase.

How long does a typical engagement take?

A single-discipline model takes 2–4 weeks. Multi-discipline coordination with clash resolution typically runs 4–8 weeks depending on building complexity and the number of revision cycles.

How are clashes detected and resolved?

We run automated clash tests in Navisworks against discipline-specific tolerances, export BCF issues, and hold weekly coordination meetings to track resolution status. Every clash gets an owner and a close-out date.

What happens when design changes arrive mid-project?

We track design changes through a formal change log. We assess the impact on LOD, schedule and clash status, issue a revised scope if needed, and update the model against the confirmed revision. No uncontrolled updates.
